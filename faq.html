The Basics

Absolutely not.

18+

If you're asking, you're probably too responsible for this game.

Officially: 3–10 players.

With 3 or more players, use the normal voting rules. Everyone plays a Reaction Card or tells a story, then the group votes for the funniest, most awkward or most painfully relatable response.

With 2 players, use 2-Player Mode instead. You both play a Reaction Card or tell a real story, then decide together who had the best response. If you cannot agree, the person who read the Experience Card chooses.

The more players involved, the more likely someone reveals something they should have taken to the grave.

More than 10 people can play unofficially, but the game gets chaotic and the Reaction deck may run low. For larger groups, either play fewer rounds, skip Story Cards, or let players create their own responses.

At that point you are no longer playing a card game. You are facilitating a support group that has completely lost control.

Anywhere from 15 minutes to several hours.

Depends how much emotional damage you're willing to inflict.
